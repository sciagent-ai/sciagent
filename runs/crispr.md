# CRISPR Gene Editing Systems Comparison

## System Comparison

| System | Efficiency | Best Use |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Cas9** | High DSB efficiency (70-90%) | Large insertions/deletions, gene knockouts |
| **Cas12a** | Moderate (60-80%), T-rich PAM | Multiplexing, staggered cuts, T-rich regions |
| **Base Editors** | High precision (50-75%), no DSB | Single nucleotide changes (C→T, A→G) |
| **Prime Editors** | Moderate (20-60%), no DSB | Precise insertions, deletions, all base changes |

## Key Findings

- **Cas9 and Cas12a** create double-strand breaks (DSBs) enabling large edits but risk off-target effects and indels, while **base editors and prime editors** avoid DSBs for safer, more precise modifications
- **Prime editing** offers the broadest editing scope without DSBs, performing all 12 base transitions and small indels, but with lower efficiency than traditional Cas9
- **Cas12a** provides unique advantages for multiplexing and recognizing T-rich PAM sequences (TTTV), complementing Cas9's G-rich PAM requirements (NGG)
